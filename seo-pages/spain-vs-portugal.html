2026 Strategic Analysis

As we enter 2026, the gap between Spain and Portugal has narrowed. Spain's **Beckham Law** remains the gold standard for simplicity, offering a flat 24% for up to 6 years with virtually no "tax residency" traps for your global assets. If you earn over €100k, Spain often wins on pure net income due to slightly lower rental inflation in tier-2 cities like Valencia or Malaga.

Portugal, however, has replaced the old NHR with the **ITS (Incentivised Tax Scheme)**. While the requirements are stricter, the 20% flat rate for 10 years is an unbeatable long-term play. The "hidden cost" in 2026 is Lisbon's rental market, which has now surpassed Madrid by nearly 12% in the nomad-friendly districts of Arroios and Santos.
